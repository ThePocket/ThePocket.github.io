How To Use A Metronome

  • Set the tempo to a moderately slow tempo (say 85bpm), and tap your foot, clap or bang something along with the beat. Try to get your taps or claps to line up exactly with the click of the metronome.
  • Try drawing out a circle in the air with your hand, and keep looping it around so you make one revolution every click. Do this in whatever way or shape feels comfortable to you. The idea is this kind of looping motion can help you feel the time in between the clicks. It is a little like dancing. When you begin to feel the groove of the metronome, you're in the right place.
  • Try find where the mid spot is between each pair of clicks. If each of these clicks is a 1/4 note, then this halfway point is an 1/8th note.
  • Play along with the metronome to improve your timing and feel on your instrument.
